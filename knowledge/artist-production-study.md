## SWEDISH HOUSE MAFIA

**Members:** Axwell, Steve Angello, Sebastian Ingrosso  
**Era:** 2008-2013, 2018-present  
**Genre:** Progressive House, Big Room, Festival Anthems

### What Makes Them Great

**1. The Big Room Sound**
- Pioneered the "stadium-sized" progressive house sound
- Massive builds and drops designed for festival main stages
- Every element serves the crowd energy

**2. Collaborative Chemistry**
- Three distinct producers with different strengths
- Axwell: Melodic genius, emotional progressions
- Steve Angello: Dark, aggressive, driving energy
- Sebastian Ingrosso: Groove, rhythm, arrangement

**3. Production Philosophy**
- "Music should make you feel something BIG"
- Focus on emotional impact over technical perfection
- Designed for maximum crowd reaction

### Signature Techniques

**Build & Release Structure:**
- Extended builds (16-32 bars)
- Tension through filtering, risers, snare rolls
- Explosive drops with full-spectrum energy
- White noise risers + pitch-rising synths

**Sound Design:**
- **Supersaw leads** - Multiple detuned saw waves, stereo-widened
- **Sidechain compression** - Heavy pumping effect (kick triggers)
- **Layered vocals** - Pitched, processed, often chopped
- **Sub bass** - Clean sine wave locked to kick

**Arrangement:**
- Intro → Build → Drop → Breakdown → Build 2 → Drop 2 → Outro
- Drop typically 16 bars, then variation or breakdown
- Second drop often more intense than first

**Effects:**
- Heavy reverb on vocals and lead elements
- Delay throws for dramatic effect
- Bitcrusher/distortion for aggression
- Gated reverb on snares

### Known Equipment/Software

**DAW:** Logic Pro X (Axwell), Ableton Live (Angello)

**Synths:**
- Sylenth1 (supersaws, pads)
- Nexus (quick sounds)
- Massive (bass, leads)
- Serum (modern productions)

**Plugins:**
- LennarDigital Sylenth1
- Waves SSL Compressor
- FabFilter Pro-Q (EQ)
- Valhalla VintageVerb

**Workflow:**
- Often start with chord progression or vocal
- Build kick and bass foundation
- Add melodic elements
- Focus on arrangement and energy flow
- Mix as they go (not separate mix stage)

### Key Tracks to Study

- "Don't You Worry Child" - Emotional progression, vocal treatment
- "Greyhound" - Aggressive synth work, energy
- "One" - Classic big room structure
- "Save The World" - Melodic hooks, crowd interaction

### What We Can Learn

✅ **Emotional Arc** - Every track tells a story through energy  
✅ **Simplicity** - Not overcomplicated, but massive impact  
✅ **Crowd Psychology** - Understanding what makes people move  
✅ **Collaborative Mindset** - Three voices = richer result  
✅ **Confidence** - Bold choices, not second-guessing

## STEVE ANGELLO

**Solo Identity:** Darker, more underground than SHM  
**Labels:** Size Records, Studio Barnhus  
**Style:** Tech House, Progressive, Experimental

### What Makes Him Great

**1. Dark Energy**
- Prefers darker, moodier sounds than typical EDM
- Influenced by techno and underground house
- Not afraid to be aggressive and abrasive

**2. Sound Design Innovation**
- Constantly experimenting with new synthesis techniques
- Layering multiple synths for one sound
- Heavy use of distortion and saturation

**3. Label Curation (Size Records)**
- A&R skills - finding and developing talent
- Understanding what makes a track work on a big system
- Business acumen combined with artistry

### Signature Techniques

**Synth Work:**
- **Distorted leads** - Clean synth → multiple distortion stages
- **Detuned layers** - 3-7 layers of same synth, slightly detuned
- **Aggressive filtering** - Resonant filters with automation
- **Industrial sounds** - Metallic hits, machinery samples

**Bass:**
- Heavier, more aggressive than typical progressive house
- Often uses techno-influenced basslines
- Sidechain compression to create space for kick
- Sub + mid-bass layering for fullness

**Drums:**
- Punchy, compressed kicks
- Layered claps/snares (sometimes 4-5 layers)
- Hi-hats with movement (filter sweeps, panning)
- Percussion loops for groove

**Vocal Processing:**
- Pitched vocals (often -12 semitones for depth)
- Heavy reverb for atmosphere
- Chopped and rearranged for rhythmic effect
- Sometimes unintelligible = becomes instrument

### Known Equipment/Software

**DAW:** Ableton Live (primary), Logic Pro

**Synths:**
- Access Virus TI
- Native Instruments Massive
- Sylenth1
- Serum
- Hardware: Moog Sub 37, Dave Smith Prophet

**Plugins:**
- SoundToys Decapitator (distortion)
- FabFilter bundle
- Waves CLA series
- Valhalla reverbs

**Workflow:**
- Often starts with a bassline or drum groove
- Builds atmosphere before adding lead elements
- Heavy experimentation with FX chains
- A/B referencing constantly

### Key Tracks to Study

- "Knas" - Dark, driving techno influence
- "SLVR" - Aggressive synth work
- "Rejoice" (feat. T.D. Jakes) - Vocal manipulation
- "Children of the Wild" - Emotional depth with darkness

### What We Can Learn

✅ **Don't Follow Trends** - Stay true to your sound  
✅ **Distortion is a Tool** - Not always clean = better  
✅ **Atmosphere Matters** - Dark doesn't mean boring  
✅ **Layering** - One synth sound can be 5 layers  
✅ **Business Mind** - Artist + entrepreneur

## FRED AGAIN..

**Real Name:** Fred Gibson  
**Era:** 2019-present (solo), longtime producer/writer before  
**Genre:** House, UK Garage, Emotional Electronic, Live Performance

### What Makes Him Great

**1. Emotional Authenticity**
- Uses real-life voice notes, field recordings, personal moments
- Music feels REAL, not manufactured
- Vulnerable and honest in songwriting

**2. Live Performance Innovation**
- Performs electronic music LIVE with hardware
- Mix of production and performance
- Energy of a DJ set + intimacy of live instruments

**3. Production Versatility**
- Produced for Ed Sheeran, Stormzy, FKA twigs, BTS, etc.
- Can do pop, hip-hop, electronic, indie
- Adapts to artist while maintaining quality

**4. Speed & Intuition**
- Known for working FAST
- Captures the moment/feeling before overthinking
- "First thought, best thought" philosophy

### Signature Techniques

**Sampling & Voice Notes:**
- Uses iPhone voice memos as primary sound sources
- Friends' conversations, phone calls, random moments
- Humanizes electronic music
- Often barely processes them - leaves raw

**Drum Programming:**
- UK Garage shuffle/swing on hi-hats
- Kick patterns that breathe (not always on 1 and 3)
- Layered percussion with real-world sounds
- Subtle ghost notes and variations

**Vocal Processing:**
- Minimal processing to maintain realness
- Pitch correction used tastefully (not T-Pain level)
- Stutter edits and chops for rhythm
- Reverb for space, delay for movement

**Bass:**
- Often garage-influenced bass patterns
- Sub-bass that's felt more than heard
- Simple but effective - serves the groove
- Sidechained to kick for clarity

**Arrangement:**
- NON-traditional structure
- Songs evolve organically
- Multiple movements within one track
- Doesn't follow EDM formula

**Hardware Integration:**
- Uses Push, drum machines, controllers LIVE
- Modular synths for organic movement
- Looping and live manipulation
- Everything is "performable"

### Known Equipment/Software

**DAW:** Ableton Live (exclusively)

**Hardware:**
- Ableton Push (central to workflow)
- Moog Subsequent 37
- Sequential Prophet
- Korg Volca series
- Various modular gear

**Plugins:**
- Valhalla VintageVerb (signature spacious sound)
- FabFilter Pro-Q 3
- Native Instruments Kontakt (samples)
- Soundtoys bundle
- iZotope RX (cleaning voice notes)

**Field Recording:**
- iPhone for capturing moments
- Zoom recorders
- Uses imperfections as character

### Workflow

1. **Capture the moment** - Record voice notes, ideas, conversations
2. **Import to Ableton** - Dump everything into session
3. **Find the vibe** - Loop interesting sections
4. **Build around it** - Add drums, bass, atmosphere
5. **Keep it raw** - Don't over-produce
6. **Arrange for emotion** - Structure follows feeling
7. **Mix minimally** - Keep the human element
8. **Test live** - See if it works in performance context

**Philosophy:**
- "Make music about your life"
- "Don't wait for perfect - capture now"
- "Electronic music can be emotional"
- "Performance > Perfection"

### Key Tracks to Study

- "Marea (We've Lost Dancing)" - Voice note usage, emotional build
- "Angie (I've Been Lost)" - UK Garage influence, arrangement
- "Delilah (pull me out of this)" - Live energy, real moments
- "Danielle (smile on my face)" - Sampling, groove

### What We Can Learn

✅ **Use Real Life** - Your life = your sound  
✅ **Don't Overthink** - First take often best  
✅ **Emotion > Technique** - Feeling matters most  
✅ **Performance Mindset** - Make it playable  
✅ **Speed is a Skill** - Fast decisions = authentic results

## CHRIS LAKE

**Era:** 2004-present (longevity!)  
**Genre:** Tech House, Jackin' House, Bass House  
**Label:** Black Book Records

### What Makes Him Great

**1. Groove Master**
- Understands what makes people dance
- Basslines that are INFECTIOUS
- Percussion that's hypnotic

**2. Consistency**
- Releasing quality for 20+ years
- Evolved with trends without losing identity
- Every release is DJ-ready

**3. Technical Excellence**
- Mixes are CLEAN but punchy
- Low-end management is perfect
- Tracks translate on any system

**4. DJ Understanding**
- Makes tracks that work in sets
- Intro/outro DJ-friendly
- Energy management within track

### Signature Techniques

**The Chris Lake Bassline:**
- **Rhythmic, not melodic** - Repeating pattern, hypnotic
- **Filtered movement** - Opens and closes over bars
- **Sidechain pumping** - Extreme ducking to kick
- **Saturation** - Adds harmonics for presence
- **Sub-bass separate** - Clean low-end underneath

**Drums:**
- **Kick:** Punchy, tight, well-EQ'd (no mud)
- **Clap/Snare:** Layered, processed, sits perfectly
- **Hi-hats:** Open and closed interplay, swing
- **Percussion:** Shakers, tambourines, congas for groove

**Vocal Hooks:**
- Short, catchy phrases
- Often pitched or processed
- Repeated strategically (not too much)
- Becomes part of the groove

**Sound Design:**
- Stabs and hits that complement bassline
- White noise for energy
- Risers that aren't cheesy
- FX that serve the groove

**Arrangement:**
- Intro (32 bars, beatless or minimal)
- Build to drop (16 bars)
- Drop (32-64 bars with variations)
- Breakdown (16-32 bars)
- Build 2
- Drop 2 (variation or filter)
- Outro (32 bars, DJ-friendly)

### Known Equipment/Software

**DAW:** Ableton Live

**Synths:**
- Serum (primary for bass)
- Massive
- Sylenth1
- Native Instruments FM8

**Samples:**
- Vengeance sample packs (drums)
- Splice
- Custom sample library built over years

**Plugins:**
- FabFilter Pro-Q 3 (surgical EQ)
- FabFilter Pro-C 2 (compression)
- Soundtoys Devil-Loc (drum compression)
- Valhalla VintageVerb
- iZotope Ozone (mastering)

**Mixing Approach:**
- High-pass everything except kick and bass
- Sidechain almost everything to kick
- Parallel compression on drums
- Stereo width on non-bass elements
- Reference tracks constantly

### Workflow

1. **Start with drums** - Get the groove right first
2. **Add bassline** - Build around kick pattern
3. **Sidechain setup** - Everything pumps with kick
4. **Add elements** - Vocals, stabs, FX
5. **Arrange** - DJ-friendly structure
6. **Mix as you go** - Don't wait until end
7. **A/B reference** - Compare to pro tracks
8. **Master** - Loudness without sacrifice

**Philosophy:**
- "The groove is everything"
- "Less is more - every element has purpose"
- "Mix for club systems"
- "Make tracks DJs want to play"

### Key Tracks to Study

- "Operator (Ring Ring)" - Iconic bassline, vocal hook
- "Turn Off The Lights" - Groove, arrangement
- "I Want You" - Tech house perfection
- "Chest" - Energy management, build/release

### What We Can Learn

✅ **Groove First** - Technical stuff serves the groove  
✅ **Less Elements, More Impact** - Quality over quantity  
✅ **Mix for the System** - Think about where it'll play  
✅ **DJ-Friendly** - Make it playable in sets  
✅ **Consistent Quality** - Every release should bang

## FLUME

**Real Name:** Harley Edward Streten  
**Era:** 2011-present  
**Genre:** Future Bass, Experimental Electronic, Sound Design

### What Makes Him Great

**1. Sound Design Innovation**
- Pioneered "future bass" genre
- Sounds nobody had heard before
- Constantly pushing boundaries

**2. Textural Approach**
- Thinks in textures, not just notes
- Organic + synthetic blend
- Imperfections as features

**3. Genre-Defying**
- Not confined to one style
- Hip-hop, pop, experimental, ambient all in one album
- Collaborates across genres successfully

**4. Technical Mastery**
- Engineering is CLEAN
- Stereo field usage is next-level
- Dynamics and space management

### Signature Techniques

**The Flume Sound:**
- **Pitched vocal chops** - Signature technique
- **Detuned supersaws** - Lush, wide, emotional
- **Organic samples** - Field recordings processed
- **Glitchy edits** - Stutter, reverse, time-stretch
- **Sub-bass presence** - Clean, powerful low-end

**Vocal Processing:**
- Pitched +12/-12 semitones for texture
- Chopped into rhythm (1/16th or 1/32nd notes)
- Formant shifting for character
- Reverb + delay for space
- Often unintelligible = becomes instrument

**Drums:**
- NOT quantized to grid - natural feel
- Layered samples (acoustic + electronic)
- Punchy but not overcompressed
- Space between hits (breathing room)
- Unconventional patterns

**Synth Work:**
- Multiple layers, slightly detuned
- Movement via LFOs and automation
- FM synthesis for metallic tones
- Granular synthesis for textures
- Heavy stereo width processing

**Sound Design Process:**
1. Start with interesting source (voice, field recording, synth)
2. Mangle it (pitch, reverse, stretch, glitch)
3. Layer processed versions
4. Add movement and modulation
5. Find the "happy accident"

**Arrangement:**
- NON-traditional structure
- Intro can be 30 seconds or 2 minutes
- Drops aren't always "drops"
- Focus on journey, not formula
- Dynamics - quiet parts are QUIET
